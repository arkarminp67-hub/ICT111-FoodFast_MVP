# Lab 03: Hypothesis Validation Matrix (Assumption vs. Evidence)

**Analyst:** Thant Zin (Product/Validation Lead)  
**Verification Method:** Empirical Survey Analytics (*N = 40*)  

---

| Core Venture Assumption | Pre-Lab 03 Risk Profile | Evidence Gathered from Target Users | Validation Status | Product Strategy Adjustment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Assumption 1:** Campus cafeteria wait times are a major issue, frequently causing students to miss meals. | **High Risk** (Foundational Problem Statement) | **72.5%** of surveyed respondents (29 out of 40) confirmed they have skipped a meal or arrived late to class due to queue delays. | **FULLY VALIDATED** | Proceed with development. The problem is a significant pain point for our target users. |
| **Assumption 2:** Physical lines during peak lunch hours frequently exceed 10 minutes. | **Medium Risk** (Quantifies the exact time lost) | **80%** of respondents wait more than 10 minutes (50% experience 11–20 min lines; 30% face lines over 20 mins). | **FULLY VALIDATED** | Anchor the application's main value proposition around saving this specific 10–20 minute window. |
| **Assumption 3:** Users want a mobile payment solution built directly into the web application. | **Low Risk** (Technical Execution) | **77.5%** of respondents select Mobile Wallets/QR as their preferred payment method. Only 7.5% prefer cash. | **FULLY VALIDATED** | Prioritize frontend development for QR integrations, moving traditional credit card fields down the pipeline. |
| **Assumption 4:** Campus vendors are deeply frustrated by manual paper ticketing systems. | **Medium Risk** (Vendor Engagement) | Observation data shows 15% of users identify manual ticket mix-ups as their main issue, but line size remains the primary bottleneck (57.5%). | **PARTIALLY VALIDATED** | Reposition the vendor dashboard UI to prioritize order queue visibility over ticket tracking. |
